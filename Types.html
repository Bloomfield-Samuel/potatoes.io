Russet Potatoes

Russet potatoes, also known as Idaho potatoes, are characterized by their rough brown skin and oval shape.
They have a fluffy texture when cooked, making them ideal for baking, mashing, and frying. Russets are high
in starch and low in moisture, which contributes to their ability to absorb flavors well. They're commonly
used in dishes like baked potatoes, french fries, and mashed potatoes due to their versatility and ability
to hold their shape during cooking.

Yukon Gold Potatoes

Yukon Gold potatoes are recognized for their smooth, golden-yellow skin and rich, buttery flavor. These
potatoes have a medium starch content and a creamy texture when cooked, making them excellent for boiling,
roasting, and mashing. Yukon Golds retain their shape well after cooking, which makes them perfect for
dishes like potato salads and gratins. Their buttery taste also adds a delightful depth to mashed potatoes
without needing excessive amounts of butter.

Red Potatoes

Red potatoes are known for their thin red skin and crisp, white flesh. They have a lower starch content
and higher moisture content compared to russet potatoes, resulting in a waxy texture that holds its shape
after cooking. Red potatoes are versatile and well-suited for roasting, boiling, and steaming. Their firm
texture makes them ideal for dishes like potato salads, soups, and stews. Additionally, their vibrant color
adds visual appeal to various recipes, making them a popular choice in the kitchen.
